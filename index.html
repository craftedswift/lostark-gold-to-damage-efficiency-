How this works

Every gold sink in Lost Ark converts to damage at a different rate, and that rate gets worse as you invest more into a system (diminishing returns / rising costs). This tool runs a simple greedy allocator: given your gold, it always buys whichever next upgrade gives you the most damage % per gold, across every system, until your budget runs out.

⚠️ The default costs and damage % values below are rough community-average estimates, not exact current-patch numbers — honing costs, gem prices, and engraving book prices shift with every patch and fluctuate by server/region. Edit any field to match your actual server prices and current progression for a real recommendation. Treat the output as a prioritization framework, not gospel.

Why these systems, and general priority logic

  • Engravings (books / accessory swaps) — usually the single biggest damage jump per gold when you're missing a key engraving level (e.g. going from 0/3 to 3/3 on a core engraving), because it can flip skills from "off" to "on" or unlock a whole damage multiplier. Very high priority when incomplete.
  • Combat / Damage & Cooldown gems — cheap at low levels, scale smoothly, and cooldown gems increase uptime on your damage buffs/identity meter, which compounds. Usually excellent early efficiency that tapers off past level 7-8.
  • Accessory quality (reform) — quality affects the substat roll on accessories; cheap to push at low quality, gets expensive near 100. Good background spend, rarely the top priority.
  • Weapon & armor honing — big flat damage/HP increases, but cost scales steeply (especially past +15/+20-equivalent breakpoints). Weapon honing generally beats armor honing for damage per gold since it applies to skill damage directly.
  • Elixirs — solid, fairly linear damage/utility gains once unlocked; compete directly with honing gold once you have a weapon/armor elixir slot open.
  • Transcendence — similar profile to elixirs/honing hybrid; efficiency depends heavily on current point count.
  • Card sets — a lump-sum unlock (not modeled as a "level" here); worth pricing separately since it's often a one-time purchase rather than a repeatable investment.

The general rule of thumb across patches: fix missing engravings first, then buy cheap gem levels and quality, then split remaining gold between honing and elixirs/transcendence using whichever has the better gold-to-% ratio at your current tier. This calculator just makes that comparison numerically instead of by feel.
